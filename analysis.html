The Health and Economic Consequences of Storms

Matthew Hayes

Synopsis

Tornados resulted in the most fatalities, both in total and on average. Most injuries come from tornados (total) and heat waves (on average). Property and crop damage share a common theme: too much water isnt good. Floods, hurricanes, and typhoons top the lists, both in total and on average. When considering combined health consequences, tornados result in the most harm by far, but on average one should be more concerned with heat waves, tropical storms, and wildfires. As for economic consequences, it cant be said enough: Water and wind are costly.

Looks like we have some data entry issues. Given this, we need to remove any records that do not match our known multipliers.

storm <- mutate(storm, propdmgexp = tolower(propdmgexp),
                cropdmgexp = tolower(propdmgexp))
good <- c("k", "m", "b", NA) 
gProp <- storm$propdmgexp %in% good
gCrop <- storm$cropdmgexp %in% good
storm$propdmgexp[!gProp] <- NA
storm$cropdmgexp[!gCrop] <- NA
rm(good, gProp, gCrop)
unique(storm$propdmgexp)
## [1] "k" "m" NA  "b"
unique(storm$cropdmgexp)
## [1] "k" "m" NA  "b"

Lets convert these multipliers to their respective numerical equivalents and use them to find the true values of property and crop damage.

storm <- storm %>%
    mutate(propdmgexp = gsub("k", 1000, propdmgexp),
           propdmgexp = gsub("m", 1000000, propdmgexp),
           propdmgexp = gsub("b", 1000000000, propdmgexp),
           cropdmgexp = gsub("k", 1000, cropdmgexp),
           cropdmgexp = gsub("m", 1000000, cropdmgexp),
           cropdmgexp = gsub("b", 1000000000, cropdmgexp),
           propdmgexp = as.numeric(propdmgexp),
           cropdmgexp = as.numeric(cropdmgexp),
           propdmg = propdmg * propdmgexp,
           cropdmg = cropdmg * cropdmgexp
    ) %>%
    select(-propdmgexp, -cropdmgexp)
